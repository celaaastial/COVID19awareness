The COVID-19 Pandemic and its Indelible Impact

The COVID-19 pandemic has been ongoing for almost two years, and although vaccine efforts may be successful, the damage it has left in its wake is truly devastating; socio-economically, financially, physically, and even mentally. It has taken 4.55 million lives as of October 2021. To understand the biological reason behind why the struggle to eliminate the virus is critical to eliminating it completely, we cannot prevent the collapse of markets, the loss of jobs, and even, unfortunately, the rise of sexual abuse due to the very strict lockdowns.


The Socio-economic Impact of the Pandemic

  • In a February 2021 survey in India, 73% of respondent migrant households have lost their jobs
  • Education all around the world has been forcibly moved online, compromising students without stable internet connections nor gadgets nor fast and reliable gadgets; learning has been compromised, especially for younger children
  • In a December 2020 report by UNDP, Sudan's food insecurity increased by 1.4 million people Click here to read the full UNDP report

The Collapse of the Market

  • On the 8th of March in 2020, Saudi Arabia raged a price war on oil with Russia. US oil prices fell by 34%, crude oil by 26%, and brent oil by 24%
  • The stock market crash lasted for a staggering more than one month from February 20 until April 7, 2020
  • The drop in remitances, especially in 3rd world countries that severely drop their already suffering economically Click here to read the crash of the stock market

The Displaceement of Students

  • By April of 2020, 1,134,916,281 were affected learners displaced from schools
  • As of October 23, 2021 there are still 55,011,070 are still affected, and although it is a great improvement, third world countries are still severely affected as to date
  • As schools are based on wifi connectivity and laptops as the switch to online learning commenced, this became an elitist route severely affecting those who cannot afford such luxury Click here to read the full UNDP report
